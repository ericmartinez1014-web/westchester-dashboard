Broker Commentary

Frozen as of July 3, 2026  ·  Data: May 2025 – Jun 2026  ·  208 closed sales  ·  Westchester (unincorporated Miami-Dade, ZIPs 33144/33155/33165/33174)

Verdict

Westchester has stabilized in the $695K–$740K median band for three consecutive months — that's a floor forming, not a freefall, and volume is quietly recovering toward normal levels.

What the Numbers Tell Me

208 closed sales from May 2025 through June 2026. Overall median sits at $711K. Average of $774K is pulled up by four outliers above $2M: the $3.95M development deal at 900 SW 80th Ct (Apr 2026), the $2.4M at 9330 SW 34th St (Oct 2025), and two new additions this month — $2.395M at 11155 SW 85th Ct and $2.037M at 11133 SW 85th Ct (both in 33156). Strip those four and the clean market median is $710K, average $736K. At $417/sqft on average, Westchester continues to offer real value density versus the broader Miami-Dade market.

Volume Trend and What It Means

June 2026 came in at 14 closings — the average pace has held at 14 sales/month for the past three months (May: 13, April: 15, June: 14), up from the prior three-month average of 12.3. After the brutal January low of 7 sales, the market has quietly stabilized. This isn't a boom, but it isn't a collapse — it's a rate-adjusted normal. At 6.43% (PMMS July 2, 2026), buyers who are qualified and motivated are transacting; the ones waiting for a return to 3% have accepted that's not happening. August 2025 remains the dataset peak at 22 closings.

Price Cuts Analysis

20 confirmed cuts out of 208 listings (9.6%) — the rate held steady from last month. Scraper coverage on this field is sparse so the real cut rate is higher. The largest cuts: 8881 SW 28 Street (-$95,001) and 7835 SW 17th St (-$90,000). No new dramatic cuts entered the dataset this month — sellers aren't panicking, which is a mild positive signal.

What I'd Tell a Seller Right Now

The $710K–$740K band is where deals are clearing for a standard 3/2. Price to that range and you'll transact within 3–4 weeks. Overpricing by even $30–40K kills your momentum in this market — stale listings attract lowball offers, not patient buyers. June's $1.12M at 7840 SW 18th Ter and $990K at 1520 SW 86th Ct show the upper end is still alive for the right product. Above $900K your pool is narrow but real buyers exist.

What I'd Tell a Buyer Right Now

At 6.70% (jumbo adjusted) on the $711K median with 20% down, you're looking at roughly $3,700/month P&I and $4,400+ PITI. Negotiating leverage is as favorable as it's been in years. Anything sitting 30+ days is fair game for a 3–5% ask reduction. I'd focus on 33155 and 33165 — most consistent volume, tightest bid-ask spreads, best resale liquidity if you need to exit in 3–5 years.

Market Analysis — Miller Method

Objective data analysis in the tradition of Jonathan Miller, Miller Samuel Inc.

Year-Over-Year Price Trend: June 2026 median of $737,750 versus June 2025 median of $742,500 — a -0.6% year-over-year change on identical volume (14 sales each month). This near-flat YoY read, combined with a recovering transaction pace, is characteristic of a market finding equilibrium after a rate-driven correction rather than one in structural decline.

Price Per Square Foot Trend: Average price per square foot in early 2026 (Jan–Jun) is $426/sqft versus $416/sqft in early 2025 — a +2.5% gain. This divergence between a flat headline median and rising $/sqft indicates a compositional shift: more smaller-footprint homes are transacting at the current price points, compressing the median while the per-unit-area value of Westchester real estate continues to appreciate modestly.

Quarterly Absorption Trend (ex-outliers): Quarterly median prices show a clear correction arc followed by stabilization — Q3 2025 peaked at $774,250 (44 sales), Q4 2025 corrected to $690,000 (54 sales, elevated by year-end clearing activity), Q1 2026 troughed at $681,250 (36 sales), and Q2 2026 recovered to $695,000 (40 sales). The Q1→Q2 recovery of +$13,750 (+2.0%) on improving volume suggests the correction has run its course at the $680–695K floor.

Price Distribution: The market is concentrated in the $600K–$800K corridor, which accounts for 51% of all transactions (107 of 208 sales). The $700–800K band alone represents 22% of volume (46 sales) — the clearest indicator of where arms-length, market-rate demand is concentrated. Sub-$500K sales (5%) likely reflect distressed transfers or condition-impaired properties. The $1M+ segment (7% combined) is active but thin, reliant on specific buyer profiles.

Market Pace: Monthly transaction velocity has recovered from the January 2026 trough of 7 sales to a 14-sale/month pace — a 100% improvement in deal flow over six months. This pace recovery in the face of 6.4%+ rates suggests the affordability ceiling has been absorbed into market expectations and buyer behavior has normalized.
